Our Story: The Journey of Learning

Sazi.life is inspired by the enduring human quest for knowledge. From ancient oral traditions to the digital age, education has shaped civilizations. This timeline traces this incredible journey, highlighting how informal learning, formal schooling, and cultural wisdom have evolved globally and within South Africa, influencing our mission to make learning accessible and meaningful for all.

Ancient Roots: Informal Learning (Prehistory - c. 3000 BCE)

Global & Africa

Knowledge passed through oral traditions, storytelling, imitation, and apprenticeship within families and small communities. Khoisan in Southern Africa master survival skills, rock art, and environmental knowledge. Bantu-speaking peoples develop agricultural techniques and iron-working through communal learning during their migrations.

Learn More

Dawn of Formal Education (c. 3000 BCE - 500 CE)

Egypt, Mesopotamia, Greece, Rome, India, China

Scribal schools in Egypt and Mesopotamia. Philosophical academies in Greece (Plato, Aristotle). Roman emphasis on rhetoric and law. Gurukuls in India. Early imperial academies in China. Focus on literacy, numeracy, philosophy, and governance for elite classes.

Learn More

Colonial Encounter & Early Formal Schooling in SA (1652 - 1800s)

South Africa

Arrival of Dutch settlers (1652). First formal school at the Cape primarily for VOC children and enslaved individuals. Missionary societies later establish schools, introducing Western education and Christianity, often clashing with indigenous learning systems. Limited access for indigenous populations.

Learn More

Industrial Revolution & Mass Schooling (18th - 19th Centuries)

Global

Rise of factories creates demand for a disciplined, literate workforce. Spread of state-sponsored elementary education in Europe and North America. Focus on standardization, discipline, and basic skills. Child labor laws begin to emerge, pushing children into schools.

Learn More

Segregation & Bantu Education (1900s - 1994)

South Africa

Formalization of racially segregated education. The Bantu Education Act (1953) institutionalizes inferior education for Black South Africans, designed to limit opportunities. Resistance from communities and alternative education initiatives (e.g., night schools, community schools) emerge.

Learn More

Post-Apartheid & Digital Age (1994 - Present)

Global & South Africa

SA transitions to democracy, aiming to redress educational imbalances. Global rise of internet and digital technologies transforms access to information. Emergence of online learning, homeschooling movements gain traction. Focus on lifelong learning, skills development, and digital literacy. Sazi.life is born from this context, seeking to bridge gaps and empower through accessible, holistic education.
